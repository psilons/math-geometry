    \begin{cbstripe}{Problem}{ctbPurpleDark}{ctbPurpleLight}
        Let H and O denote the orthocenter and circumcenter of acute triangle
        ABC, respectively. $\overline{AH}$ meets $\overline{BC}$ at P and
        $\overline{AO}$ meets $BC$ at Q. Prove
        that $\angle BAP = \angle CAQ$.
    \end{cbstripe}

    \begin{center}
    \begin{tikzpicture}[scale=1]

    First, $\angle TAC = \angle TBC = \widearc{TC}$ and so we just need to prove
    $\angle TBC = \angle BAP$.

    Next, $\angle TBA = 90\degree$ because $AT$ is a diameter of the circle, and with
    $\angle APB = 90\degree$ we conclude that $\angle TBC = \angle BAP$ because both
    adds $\angle PWB$ to $90\degree$.

    Therefore, $\angle BAP = \angle TBC = \angle TAC$.
